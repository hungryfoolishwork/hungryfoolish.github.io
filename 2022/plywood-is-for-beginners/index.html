Plywood is for Beginners

Wednesday, February 23, 2022

NEVADA CITY — Plywood is wonderful. You can trust with almost certainty it’s flat and square which jumpstarts your adventures (baltic birch is your friend). There are gobs of YouTubers making cool things with plywood. I could not stop watching Chris Salomone build all kinds of furniture, have no idea how I found him but he does a great job inspiring by way of talking through the process. Cristiana at Get Hands Dirty has some great builds as well that are almost meditative to sit through. YouTube is filled with this stuff. It’s also filled with tool reviews.

The urge to start buying tools happens quickly. After watching many tool reviews it became clear few could be trusted. Everyone is either sponsored by Woodpecker, Rockler or Kreg — all great brands but often overpriced and unnecessary. I started out with some essentials to make picture frames and prototype some furniture.

It became pretty clear the first thing I needed was a table saw. Now, I could have bought a very affordable yellow branded saw but did the math and realized a trip to the emergency room (along with pain and suffering) wasn’t worth skimping. So I bought the SawStop jobsite saw, not cheap but it will refuse to saw off your fingers and cut wood like butter. I also like how easily it folds up as I have very little space to persue this passion.

The next tools I picked up were a good cordless drill and driver. I went with Metabo because I saw a lot of construction workers using their stuff. It’s good to be consistent because you’ll want the freedom to swap batteries across power tools. At the time Metabo was the only brand with a multi-volt battery option which seemed beneficial down the road.

The next few things I bought were not necessary and I regret spending so much money on jigs that make it easy to cut miters or small pieces of material. When you watch people use these damn things it’s hard to resist, I have learned my lesson. The last thing I bought was a combination square. Spent too much money on a trendy red aluminum thingy when I should have just bought a Starrett.

With those tools I was able to build some nice picture frames out of elm. After getting comfortable with the table saw I went on to build very basic furniture out of cheap plywood. It’s not furniture I’m proud of but it’s functional and helping me take baby steps towards the more serious world of hand tools and joinery.
