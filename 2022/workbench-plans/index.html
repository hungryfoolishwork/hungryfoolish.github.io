Plans are in a good place, spent many evenings watching YouTube and reading Christopher Schwarz’s Anarchist’s Workbench. Everything has been helpful to understand the craft and tactics involved in construction.

Getting into woodworking for me, specifically hand-tool woodworking, is meant to be a reprieve from the computer. Plans could be made entirely on paper but my mind is so used to thinking at the speed of software like Sketch or Figma it’s hard to discard it completely. SketchUp, the tool most amateurs use to model in three dimensions was an option but I have a long hatred of modeling software. It’s always felt fussy and too easy to get lost within. I don’t really feel the need to spin things around in three dimensions on a screen, I can do that pretty well in my own mind. Having a way to do isometric sketches would be as close to 3d as I’m willing to go.

A little about the bench, it’s inspired by the Curtis Buchanan bench Bibbings & Hensby built recently. A chairmakers workbench has a smaller top and is a little deeper. Using cheap and readily available Douglas Fur. No electric planer or jointer means I’ll be relying entirely on hand planes to wrestle the boards to be flat and square. The top is laminated which took a few evenings to get flat (felt more like therapy than work). There leg vice will use Benchcrafted hardware and there will be a series of bench dogs in front of the vice. I’d like to use a couple holdfasts but unsure about placement. Most holdfasts I’ve found are 1" in diameter rather than 3/4". Is it typical to have different sized holes for bench dogs and holdfasts?
